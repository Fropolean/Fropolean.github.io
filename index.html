- About Me -

I grew up in Eagle River, Alaska, of all places. When I graduated from high school I went straight to the local university for music education, thinking that my tenure as the band council president had prepared me for a long and happy career in music education. Spoiler alert: it wasn't all that cracked up to be. So! I did something I never thought I'd ever do in my life: dropped out of college. And it was liberating! But pretty boring after a couple months. School had been a staple of my life since I was five, and here I was with an enormous void. Restlessness spawned wanderlust and for the next couple years I backpacked, grew up, moved to Anchorage, taught myself Ruby out of boredom, and decided to get in on the tech boom. Now I'm at Epicodus! There isn't really a plan for what to do when the program ends, but I'll figure out some way to apply my new, highly employable skill set.

More fun facts about me: I'm a vegetarian, sunburn easily, eat lemons sometimes, am a feminist, and I play the bassoon!

- Projects -

Vacation Planner

The second code review for Epicodus. This is a tool that helps users decide on a vacation spot based on their input. The JS is relatively simple and employs several nested conditional statements and a count to determine the outcome.

Frogs

Made in the first week of Epicodus. This is a webpage about frogs from around the world. It has columns, an image carousel, and an embedded video. For this project we used HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap. I uh, I really like frogs.

Hangman

This is a pretty basic browser app of the classic hangman game. The construction is fairly simple and consists of two main functions. The hangman image is derived from an array that displays a new string every time a letter is missed.
